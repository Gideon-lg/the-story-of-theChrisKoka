A Year at Irembo: Reflections on Enterprise Product Management

2024-06-01

“One of the most valuable skills in life is being able to see another person’s perspective. If you’re going to someone’s house, think about how it might feel to be the host. If you’re creating a product, spend as much time as possible thinking like the customer.” — James Clear

“The more clearly you understand the viewpoint of your spouse or customer or coworker, the better positioned you are to find a solution.” — James Clear

The month of May marked a significant milestone for me: one year as a Senior Product Manager at Irembo, where I have had the privilege of leading IremboPay, Irembo’s payment gateway. Reflecting on this journey, I realize how much I have grown both professionally and personally. From diving into the world of fintech to relocating to Rwanda, the past year has been an amazing journey and adventure filled with lots of learning and growth.

Relocating To Rwanda: A New Chapter

Relocating to Rwanda was a big decision that brought a mix of excitement and apprehension. Moving from West Africa to East Africa! From spices &pepper to zero spices &zero pepper! From heat to cold! From fufu to ugali! From jollof to pilau! Moving to a new country always comes with its challenges, but Rwanda quickly felt like home. The turning point came when a colleague saw me enjoying Rwandan food with a smile. It was a stark contrast to how I felt when I first arrived. That’s when it hit me; my taste buds had finally adjusted to the zero-spice Rwandan cuisine! My transition was smoother and more enriching than I could have imagined.

Intrigued by my move to Rwanda? We will get to that later! For now, let’s dive into my reflections on product management in the past year.

Embracing Fintech &Enterprise Product Management

Transitioning to fintech is one thing and transitioning to enterprise product management is another thing and here I was, doing both at the same time! Good Jesus!

T he fintech learning curve was a lot! I found myself learning new things like payouts, invoices, settlements, transit account, escrow account, settlement flow, T-2, T+1, principal accounts, pricing rules, merchants, payment account identifiers, etc.

As a newcomer to the fintech space, I knew I needed a strong foundation to excel in my new role. Thankfully, I had the opportunity to connect with exceptional mentors, Alex Abbey , Emile (PM &Data Engineer, Fido) & Patrick (Head Of Product, Hubtel)

Through dedicated calls and in-depth discussions, they equipped me with the essential terms, concepts, and the overall structure of the fintech industry.

Taking the time to learn from these mentors proved invaluable during my probation period. It laid the foundation for me to understand the importance of product and organizational knowledge!

Reflection 1 — The Power Of Mentorship: Whenever you are transitioning into a new role, make sure you have people in similar industries who can help you navigate the process of understanding your product, industry and organization.

E mbracing enterprise product management was another learning curve. I am grateful to my manager at Irembo for helping me on this journey. During one of our 1:1s, he explained the differences and challenges between enterprise products and consumer products. He highlighted approaching product management from the enterprise perspective as I had the tendency of approaching it from the consumer perspective. He recommended “Building Products For The Enterprise” by Reeves &Gaines which was a game-changer to the way I approached product management.

Let me briefly touch on enterprise product management.

According to Reeves &Gaines, there are three big elements that make product management in enterprise software different: “our business model, our specialization, and the split between our customers and users.” Enterprise product management involves your company building products for other enterprises or organizations.

The business model operates almost exclusively on a direct-sales mode and relies on direct sales to sell complicated products and solutions to a relatively small (compared to consumer software) group of sophisticated customers.

Enterprise software is also very specialized and tailored to serving a specific technical or business need. Think BambooHR , tailored to meet the needs of human resource departments in organizations.

In enterprise product management, your customers are the organizations that pay for your product while your users are the people within the organizations that use your product.

Your users work directly with your product to perform some function that allows them to do their jobs better, more easily, or efficiently while your customers, by contrast, are nearly always those users’ bosses (or their bosses, or even their bosses’ bosses) who write the cheque and the goal is to make sure that your customers (the cheque writers) are happy!

Reflection 2 — Moving Fast vs Building Right : One key thing I learned from Reeves &Gaines was “move fast and break things is a recipe for disaster in enterprise software.”

With consumer software, it is all about speed, but with enterprise, the focus is more on building the right product and building it right, and this takes time.

Emotions Outside The Window!

The journey with the team has also been an amazing one. I thought I was a patient soul, but boy! I had to learn to keep my emotions outside the window!

I am grateful to my colleagues for teaching me patience! Brainstorming sessions were very emotional and it was during my 1:1 with a colleague that we discussed the importance of putting emotions aside and always focusing on what’s best for the product and the company.

Blair &Reeves, in their book, emphasize that you make the best decision for the product by constantly asking yourself; “is this the best decision for the product?” This makes all the difference in the way the product manager approaches problems and solutions.

The Journey Ahead

As I look back on the past year, I am proud of what we have accomplished with IremboPay. We have made significant strides in enhancing our payment gateway, we have solved customer problems with amazing features &improvements that have contributed to achieving business objectives &revenue growth!

The journey ahead is filled with exciting possibilities. Irembo is committed to continuing its mission to make financial services accessible to all and to drive digital transformation in Rwanda. Personally, I am eager to keep learning, growing, and contributing to this vibrant industry.

Customer problems are the “currency” of product management . The better you understand them, the richer you are.

Conclusion

My first year at Irembo has been a remarkable journey of discovery and growth. From embracing the challenges of fintech and enterprise product management, to finding a new home in Rwanda, every experience has been a stepping stone towards becoming a better leader and product manager. I appreciate the opportunity to be a part of IremboPay and I’m excited for the future as we continue to innovate and make a difference.

To anyone considering a leap into fintech or a relocation to a new country, I say embrace the journey. The challenges will shape you, and the experiences will be incredibly rewarding. Here’s to many more milestones and achievements in the years to come!
